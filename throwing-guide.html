How to Throw a Football

Throwing Normally

To throw a football normally, follow these basic steps:

  1. Grip: Hold the ball with your fingers across the laces, with your thumb underneath and your index finger on top.
  2. Stance: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and body facing sideways to your target.
  3. Arm Motion: Bring your throwing arm back, and then snap it forward, releasing the ball with your fingers to give it a good spiral.
  4. Follow Through: After releasing the ball, your arm should follow through, finishing by pointing your thumb down and your fingers up.

Throwing Far

To throw the ball farther, you need to focus on power, precision, and technique. Here's how:

  1. Strong Grip: Your grip should be firm but not too tight. You want the ball to come off your fingers cleanly for maximum distance.
  2. Use Your Legs: Your legs play a big role in generating power. Make sure to step forward with your opposite foot as you throw the ball.
  3. Wind-Up: A bigger wind-up will help you generate more power. Rotate your torso and shift your weight onto your back foot.
  4. Throw With Speed: The key to a long throw is speed. Generate as much speed as possible with your arm and torso rotation, and release the ball when you're at your peak.
